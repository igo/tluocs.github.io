I am a Scientist and Principal Investigator at the Institute for Infocomm Research (I2R), A*STAR. I received my PhD degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from National University of Singapore (NUS) in 2009, after which I spent a year in the industry in 2010. I was a research fellow of the Computer Networks & Distributed Systems (CNDS) Lab at NUS from late 2008 to early 2010, and a research fellow of the Communications & Networks Lab (CNL) at NUS from 2011 to March 2012.

Research Interests

  • Crowdsourcing and Participatory sensing
  • Internet of things (IoT) and Cyber-physical systems (CPS)
  • Network economics
  • Wireless sensor networks

  6. Quality of Contributed Service and Market Equilibrium for Participatory Sensing
    Cheng-Khong Tham and Tie Luo
    IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing (TMC), to appear. (DOI: 10.1109/TMC.2014.2330302)
    [This paper extends my DCOSS'13 paper.]
    Abs2: We propose a metric called Quality of Contributed Service (QCS) to characterize information quality and time sensitivity of participatory sensing data. We prove QCS can converge to equilibrium under a market-based model.

  7. Fairness and Social Welfare in Service Allocation Schemes for Participatory Sensing
    Cheng-Khong Tham and Tie Luo
    Computer Networks (ComNet), Elsevier, vol. 73, pp. 58-71, November 2014.
    [This paper extends my SECON'12 paper.]
    Abs2: This paper proposes two incentive schemes called IDF and ITF to maximize fairness and social welfare, respectively. A demand-supply model is assumed where each user must supply (contribute) data in order to meet his demand for service produced from user-contributed data.

  8. Optimal Prizes for All-Pay Contests in Heterogeneous Crowdsourcing
    Tie Luo, Salil Kanhere, Sajal Das, and Hwee-Pink Tan
    The 11th IEEE International Conference on Mobile Ad-hoc and Sensor Systems (MASS), October 2014.
    Acceptance rate: 26.5% (48 out of 181)
    Abs2: This paper deals with heterogeneous users using an asymmetric all-pay contest model, of which an optimal version is designed using a prize tuple. A counter-intuitive property called strategy autonomy (SA) was newly discovered.

  9. WiFiScout: A Crowdsensing WiFi Advisory System with Gamification-based Incentive
    Fang-Jing Wu and Tie Luo
    The 11th IEEE International Conference on Mobile Ad-hoc and Sensor Systems (MASS), Demo paper, October 2014.

  10. SEW-ing a Simple Endorsement Web to incentivize trustworthy participatory sensing   [Slides]
    Tie Luo, Salil Kanhere, and Hwee-Pink Tan
    The 11th IEEE International Conference on Sensing, Communication, and Networking (SECON), July 2014, pp. 636-644.
    Acceptance rate: 28.6% (67 out of 234)
    Abs2: Taking a socio-economic approach, we propose to connect participants into a social network overlaid by economic incentives, in order to tackle the dual challenge of both incentive and trustworthiness.

  11. Profit-Maximizing Incentive for Participatory Sensing
    Tie Luo, Hwee-Pink Tan, and Lirong Xia
    The 33rd IEEE International Conference on Computer Communications (INFOCOM), April 2014, pp. 127-135.
    Acceptance rate: 19% (319 out of 1650)
    Abs2: An incentive mechanism based on all-pay auctions is designed under a generic setting: incomplete information, uncertain number of agents, and risk-averse agents. A contribution-dependent prize is used to maximize profit for the sensing campaign organizer.

  12. A Generic Participatory Sensing Framework for Multi-modal Datasets

Research Projects

  • Generalized Participatory Sensing (g-PS)
    Role: PI
    15 August 2013 -- 14 August 2016, Institute for Infocomm Research, A*STAR, Singapore
    Outcome: Our team has developed a crowdsourcing-based mobile application called WiFi-Scout, available on Google Play for free download. It allows smartphone users to search for "good" WiFi hotspots based on the quality of experience (QoE) rated by users like you and me. It integrates the theoretical outcome of Simple Endorsement Web (IEEE SECON'14) to provide incentives and improve trustworthiness.
