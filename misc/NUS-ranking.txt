[2012/13 - THE]
NUS clinches 29th place in THE World University Rankings - 04 October 2012
(Full article: http://newshub.nus.edu.sg/headlines/1012/rankings_04Oct12.php)

NUS has risen to 29th position, up from last year's 40th spot, in the 2012/2013 Times Higher Education (THE) World University Rankings. 

In terms of the rankings by subject, NUS fared well in most subjects, staying within the top 50 placement: Engineering & Technology (12th), Life Sciences (33th), Clinical, Pre-clinical & Health Universities (37th), Physical Sciences (39th), Social Sciences (39th) and Arts & Humanities (43th).

(Full results: http://www.timeshighereducation.co.uk/world-university-rankings/2012-13/world-ranking)

[2012/13 - QS]
NUS among top 25 in QS World University Rankings - 11 September 2012
(Full article: http://newshub.nus.edu.sg/headlines/0912/rankings_11Sep12.php)

NUS has risen three spots to 25 in the 2012/2013 Quacquarelli Symonds (QS) World University Rankings, from its 28th position last year. 

NUS has improved in nearly all the indicators measured by QS, including the number of research papers published and citations generated. The University has been named 9th globally for academic reputation and 14th for employer reputation. Improvements were also made in faculty areas as follows: Arts & Humanities (11), Engineering & Technology (9), Life Sciences & Medicine (11), Natural Sciences (13) and Social Sciences & Management (10). 

[2012 - QS Asia]
NUS ranked top 2 university in Asia - 29 May 2012
(Full article: http://newshub.nus.edu.sg/headlines/0512/ranked_29May12.php)

NUS has secured the number two spot in the latest 2012 Quacquarelli Symonds (QS) University Rankings: Asia, one place up from last year. This is its best performance in the QS University Rankings: Asia which was introduced in 2009. 

This year, the University put up a strong showing across most of the indicators, ranking 1st in Asia for employer reputation and international faculty, and 2nd in Asia for academic reputation.

NUS also enhanced its rankings for three faculty areas: Arts and Humanities (1st in Asia); Engineering and Technology (2nd in Asia); and Natural Sciences (2nd in Asia). It maintains the top position in Asia for Social Sciences and Management.
