Biosketch

I am a Scientist and Principal Investigator at the Institute for Infocomm Research (I2R), A*STAR, Singapore. I received my PhD degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from National University of Singapore in 2009. I was a Research Fellow of the Computer Networks & Distributed Systems Lab at NUS, subsequently worked in the industry, and then was a Postdoctoral Fellow of the Communications & Networks Lab at NUS. Since 2012 till present, I am with I2R, A*STAR.

Research Interests

  • Mobile crowdsourcing; Social sensing and computing
  • Incentive mechanisms; Trust and privacy
  • Cyber-physical systems
  • Internet of Things
  • Network economics
  • Earlier research: Wireless ad hoc and sensor networks, cognitive radio, smart grid, software-defined networking.

Selected Publications (full list of publications)

Abs2 - Abstract of Abstract.

[1. Crowdsourcing, Participatory Sensing, Mobile Crowd Sensing]

  • Incentive mechanism design for heterogeneous crowdsourcing using all-pay contests
    T. Luo, S. S. Kanhere, S. K. Das, and H.-P. Tan
    IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing (TMC), 2016 (forthcoming). Preprint available.
    DOI: 10.1109/TMC.2015.2485978

  • Crowdsourcing with Tullock Contests: A New Perspective
    T. Luo, S. S. Kanhere, H-P. Tan, F. Wu, and H. Wu
    The 34th IEEE International Conference on Computer Communications (INFOCOM), April 2015, pp. 2515-2523.
    Acceptance rate: 19%
    Best Paper Award nominee (0.3%; 5 out of 1640)
    Abs2: While auctions have dominated the realm of mechanism design for decades, we would however suggest Tullock contests as a "user-friendly" alternative. What is a Tullock contest? You can think of it as a lottery or lucky draw, in which you always have a chance to win (no matter how "weak" you are).

  • Quality of Contributed Service and Market Equilibrium for Participatory Sensing
    C-K. Tham and T. Luo
    IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing (TMC), vol 14, no. 4, pp. 829-842, April 2015.
    Abs2: The main objective of this work is to characterize QoS for crowd sensing. For this we propose a metric called Quality of Contributed Service (QCS) which aggregates individual quality of contributions and takes into account information quality and time sensitivity. This metric is then analyzed using a market based supply-and-demand model.

  • Fairness and Social Welfare in Service Allocation Schemes for Participatory Sensing
    C-K. Tham and T. Luo
    Computer Networks (COMNET), Elsevier, vol. 73, pp. 58-71, November 2014.
    Abs2: To incentivize user participation in crowd sensing, this work takes a resource-allocation approach. Specifically, it allocates service quota to users based on their contribution levels and service demands.

  • Optimal Prizes for All-Pay Contests in Heterogeneous Crowdsourcing
    T. Luo, S. Kanhere, S. Das, and H-P. Tan
    The 11th IEEE International Conference on Mobile Ad-hoc and Sensor Systems (MASS), October 2014, pp. 136-144.
    Acceptance rate: 26.5% (48 out of 181)
    Abs2: By heterogeneous, we mean that not only the player types (abilities, costs) but also the beliefs about their respective types are different. For this setting, we design an incentive mechanism using an asymmetric all-pay contest (or auction) model.

  • SEW-ing a Simple Endorsement Web to incentivize trustworthy participatory sensing
    T. Luo, S. Kanhere, and H-P. Tan
    The 11th IEEE International Conference on Sensing, Communication, and Networking (SECON), July 2014, pp. 636-644.
    Acceptance rate: 28.6% (67 out of 234)
    Abs2: This paper deals with two issues in crowd sensing: incentives and trust. It takes a socio-economic approach to connect participants into a social network overlaid by economic incentives, via a relationship called endorsement.
    (SEW has been implemented in an Android-based crowdsensing app called WiFi-Scout, available for free download.)

  • Profit-Maximizing Incentive for Participatory Sensing
    T. Luo, H-P. Tan, and L. Xia
    The 33rd IEEE International Conference on Computer Communications (INFOCOM), April 2014, pp. 127-135.
    Acceptance rate: 19% (319 out of 1650)
    Abs2: This paper proposes to use all-pay auctions to design incentive mechanisms for crowd sensing. The proposed mechanism maximizes the organizer's profit using a contribution-dependent prize under a general setting (incomplete information, risk-averse agents, and stochastic population).

[2. Cyber-Physical Systems, Internet of Things, Software-Defined Networking, Smart Grid]

  • Sensing-Driven Energy Purchasing in Smart Grid Cyber-Physical System
    C-K. Tham and T. Luo
    IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics: Systems (SMC), vol. 43, no. 4, pp. 773-784, July 2013.
    Abs2: An optimizer and decision maker is designed for each household to optimize their electricity purchase from the main grid. The optimization takes sensing data as input and uses multi-stage stochastic programming as well as linear programming.

  • Sensor OpenFlow: Enabling Software-Defined Wireless Sensor Networks
    T. Luo, H-P. Tan, and T. Q. S. Quek
    IEEE Communications Letters (CommLett), vol 16, No 11, pp. 1896-1899, November 2012.
    Abs2: This work rerepresents the first attempt to apply software-defined networking (SDN) to wireless sensor networks, by proposing a SD-WSN architecture with a Sensor OpenFlow (SOF) protocol.

  • Enhancing Responsiveness and Scalability for OpenFlow Networks via Control-Message Quenching
    T. Luo, H-P. Tan, P. C. Quan, Y-W. Law, and J. Jin
    International Conference on ICT Convergence (ICTC), October 2012, pp. 348-353.
    Best Paper Award (0.7%; 3 out of 423)
    Abs2: We identify certain (likely) SDN scenarios in which the OpenFlow switch-controller communication overhead can be substantial. We then propose a solution called CMQ, which tactically suppresses a large number of unnecessary control messages (packet-in from switches and packet-out / flow-mod from the controller).

[3. Wireless (ad hoc, sensor, cognitive radio, mesh) Networks]

  • Energy-Efficient Strategies for Cooperative Multi-Channel MAC Protocols
    T. Luo, M. Motani, and V. Srinivasan
    IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing (TMC), vol. 11, no. 4, pp. 553-566, April 2012. [Bib]
    Abs2: In the literature, a common approach to energy efficiency is to duty-cycle existing nodes (i.e., let they follow some sleep-wake schedule). This paper, on the other hand, deploys extra "altruistic" nodes which always stay awake to wake up other "normal" nodes when necessary. Counter-intuitively, this is found to be more energy- and cost- efficient.

  • A Metric for DISH Networks: Analysis, Implications, and Applications
    T. Luo, V. Srinivasan, and M. Motani
    IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing (TMC), vol. 9, no. 3, pp. 376-389, March 2010. [Bib]
    [Extended version of MobiHoc'08 paper.]

  • Cooperative Asynchronous Multichannel MAC: Design, Analysis, and Implementation
    T. Luo, M. Motani, and V. Srinivasan
    IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing (TMC). Vol. 8, no. 3, pp. 338-352, March 2009. [Bib]
    Remark: The protocol (CAM-MAC) proposed in this paper was subsequently implemented by Shashi R. Singh on a Mesh Testbed demonstrated at MobiCom'10 (program). The detailed results are reported in MobiCom'10 WiNTECH workshop (program).

  • Analyzing DISH for multi-channel MAC protocols in wireless networks
    T. Luo, M. Motani, and V. Srinivasan
    ACM International Symposium on Mobile Ad Hoc Networking and Computing (MobiHoc), May 2008, pp. 43-52. [Bib]
    • Acceptance rate: 14.7% (44 out of 300)
    Abs2: This paper offers an example of how to analyze cooperative procotols, especially in multi-hop networks which is generally difficult. This is achieved by introducing a new metric pco, which is the availability of cooperation, or the probability of obtaining cooperation.

  • Altruistic Cooperation for Energy-Efficient Multi-Channel MAC Protocols
    T. Luo, M. Motani, and V. Srinivasan
    ACM International Conference on Mobile Computing and Networking (MobiCom), September 2007. [Bib]
    • Acceptance rate: 17% (26 regular papers + 14 extended abstracts out of 233 full-paper submissions)
