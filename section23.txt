		C-K. Tham and T. Luo<br>

		<font color=Gray>Abs<sup>2</sup>: This work rerepresents the first attempt to apply <i>software-defined networking</i> (SDN) to wireless sensor networks, by proposing a SD-WSN architecture with a <i>Sensor OpenFlow</i> (SOF) protocol.</font></li><p>

	<li><a href="pub/[ICTC12]-OpenFlow-performance.pdf">Enhancing Responsiveness and Scalability for OpenFlow Networks via Control-Message Quenching</a><br>
		T. Luo, H-P. Tan, P. C. Quan, Y-W. Law, and J. Jin<br>
		<i>International Conference on ICT Convergence</i> (ICTC), October 2012, pp. 348-353.<br><!--South Korea, October 15-17,-->
		<a href="pub/[ICTC12]-BestPaperAward.pdf"><b>Best Paper Award</b></a><br><!--(0.7%; 3 out of 423)-->
		<font color=Gray>Abs<sup>2</sup>: We identify certain (likely) SDN scenarios in which the OpenFlow switch-controller communication overhead can be substantial. We then propose a solution called CMQ, which tactically suppresses a large number of unnecessary control messages (<i>packet-in</i> from switches and <i>packet-out</i> / <i>flow-mod</i> from the controller).</font></li><p><!--, resulting in a much more efficient flow setup process-->
