Biosketch

I am a Scientist and Principal Investigator at the Institute for Infocomm Research (I2R), A*STAR, Singapore. I received my PhD degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from National University of Singapore in 2009. I was a Research Fellow of the Computer Networks & Distributed Systems Lab at NUS, subsequently worked in the industry, and then was a Postdoctoral Fellow of the Communications & Networks Lab at NUS. Since 2012 till present, I am with I2R, A*STAR.

Research Interests

  • Human-centric mobile sensing and computing; Mobile crowdsourcing and ubiquitous computing
  • Cyber-physical systems
  • Internet of Things
  • Network economics
  • Earlier research: Wireless ad hoc and sensor networks, cognitive radio, smart grid, software-defined networking.

  5. Crowdsourcing with Tullock Contests: A New Perspective
    T. Luo, S. S. Kanhere, H-P. Tan, F. Wu, and H. Wu
    The 34th IEEE International Conference on Computer Communications (INFOCOM), April 2015, pp. 2515-2523.
    Acceptance rate: 19%
    Best Paper Award nominee (0.3%; 5 out of 1640)
    Abs2: What are Tullock contests? For simplicity, think of lucky draws or lotteries - "You always have a chance to win" (no matter how "weak" you are). This paper brings Tullock contests into the realm of mechanism design, which has been dominated by auctions for long, as an alternative for mechanism deigners to (seriously) consider.

    D. Peng, S. Yang, F. Wu, G. Chen, S. Tang, and T. Luo

    Acceptance rate: 19% (316 out of 1640)

  7. An Endorsement-based Reputation System for Trustworthy Crowdsourcing
    C. Wu, T. Luo, F. Wu, G. Chen
    The 34th IEEE International Conference on Computer Communications (INFOCOM) Student Workshop, April 2015, pp. 89-90.

  8. Quality of Contributed Service and Market Equilibrium for Participatory Sensing
    C-K. Tham and T. Luo
    IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing (TMC), vol 14, no. 4, pp. 829-842, April 2015.
    Abs2: We propose a metric called Quality of Contributed Service (QCS) to characterize the QoS in crowd sensing. The metric takes into account information quality and time sensitivity, and aggregates qualities of individual user contributions. We show that QCS can converge to a good market equilibrium, under a market-based supply-and-demand model when users start from purely random behaviors.
    [This is the extension of the DCOSS'13 paper (item 3 below).]

  9. A crowdsourced WiFi sensing system with an endorsement network in smart cities
    F-J. Wu, T. Luo, and Jason Cheah
    IEEE 10th International Conference on Intelligent Sensors, Sensor Networks and Information Processing (ISSNIP). April 2015, pp. 1-2. (Demo)

  10. Fairness and Social Welfare in Service Allocation Schemes for Participatory Sensing
    C-K. Tham and T. Luo
    Computer Networks (COMNET), Elsevier, vol. 73, pp. 58-71, November 2014.
    Abs2: This paper takes a resource-allocation approach to incentivize user participation by allocating service quota to users based on their contribution levels and, particularly, their service demands. Two incentive schemes, IDF and ITF, are proposed to maximize fairness and social welfare, respectively.
    [This is the extension of the SECON'12 paper (item 2 below).]

  11. Optimal Prizes for All-Pay Contests in Heterogeneous Crowdsourcing
    T. Luo, S. Kanhere, S. Das, and H-P. Tan
    The 11th IEEE International Conference on Mobile Ad-hoc and Sensor Systems (MASS), October 2014, pp. 136-144.
    Acceptance rate: 26.5% (48 out of 181)
    Abs2: An incentive mechanism is designed for heterogeneous crowdsourcing, where not only the player types (abilities, costs) but also the beliefs about their respective types are different. We cast the problem as an asymmetric all-pay contest and use a series of reward functions to achieve optimal mechanism design.

  12. WiFiScout: A Crowdsensing WiFi Advisory System with Gamification-based Incentive

    The 11th IEEE International Conference on Mobile Ad-hoc and Sensor Systems (MASS), October 2014, pp. 533-534. (Demo)

  13. A smartphone-based WiFi monitoring system

  14. SEW-ing a Simple Endorsement Web to incentivize trustworthy participatory sensing
    T. Luo, S. Kanhere, and H-P. Tan
    The 11th IEEE International Conference on Sensing, Communication, and Networking (SECON), July 2014, pp. 636-644.
    Acceptance rate: 28.6% (67 out of 234)
    Abs2: This paper takes a socio-economic approach to achieve the (dual) objective of incentivizing trustworthy crowdsensing. The core idea is to connect participants via a relationship called endorsement, into a social network overlaid by economic incentives.
    [website]

  15. Profit-Maximizing Incentive for Participatory Sensing
    T. Luo, H-P. Tan, and L. Xia
    The 33rd IEEE International Conference on Computer Communications (INFOCOM), April 2014, pp. 127-135.
    Acceptance rate: 19% (319 out of 1650)
    Abs2: An all-pay auction based incentive mechanism is proposed to achieve profit-maximization for crowd sensing. It features (1) a contribution-dependent prize and (2) a general setting that consists of incomplete information, risk-averse agents, and stochastic population.

[2. Cyber-Physical Systems, Internet of Things, Software-Defined Networking, Smart Grid]

  1. Sensing-Driven Energy Purchasing in Smart Grid Cyber-Physical System
    C-K. Tham and T. Luo
    IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics: Systems (SMC), vol. 43, no. 4, pp. 773-784, July 2013.
    Abs2: An optimizer and decision maker is designed for each household to optimize their electricity purchase from the main grid. The optimization takes sensing data as input and uses multi-stage stochastic programming as well as linear programming.

  2. Comparative study of multicast authentication schemes with application to wide-area measurement system

  3. Sensor OpenFlow: Enabling Software-Defined Wireless Sensor Networks
    T. Luo, H-P. Tan, and T. Q. S. Quek
    IEEE Communications Letters (CommLett), vol 16, No 11, pp. 1896-1899, November 2012.
    Abs2: This work rerepresents the first attempt to apply software-defined networking (SDN) to wireless sensor networks, by proposing a SD-WSN architecture with a Sensor OpenFlow (SOF) protocol.

  4. Enhancing Responsiveness and Scalability for OpenFlow Networks via Control-Message Quenching
    T. Luo, H-P. Tan, P. C. Quan, Y-W. Law, and J. Jin
    International Conference on ICT Convergence (ICTC), October 2012, pp. 348-353.
    Best Paper Award (0.7%; 3 out of 423)
    Abs2: In SDN, the OpenFlow switch-controller communication overhead can rise substantially in certain (likely) scenarios that we identify. To address this problem, we propose a CMQ algorithm which tactically suppresses a large number of unnecessary control messages (packet-in from switches and packet-out / flow-mod from the controller).

[3. Wireless (ad hoc, sensor, cognitive radio, mesh) Networks]

  1. Multi-Channel Directional Medium Access Control for Ad Hoc Networks: A Cooperative Approach
    Y. Wang, M. Motani, H. K. Garg, Q. Chen, and T. Luo

  2. Energy-Efficient Strategies for Cooperative Multi-Channel MAC Protocols
    T. Luo, M. Motani, and V. Srinivasan
    IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing (TMC), vol. 11, no. 4, pp. 553-566, April 2012. [Bib]
    Abs2: In a (common) approach to energy efficiency, existing nodes follow some sleep-wake duty-cycles; in another, extra nodes called "altruists" are added to monitor the control channel (constantly) and wake up normal nodes when necessary. Counter-intuitively, we find that the second approach turns is more efficient in both energy and cost.
    [This is the (substantial) extension of the MobiCom'07 paper (item 2 below).]

Research Projects

  • Generalized Participatory Sensing (g-PS)
    15 August 2013 -- 14 August 2016, SERC, A*STAR, Singapore.
    Out of this project, a crowdsourcing-based mobile app called "WiFi-Scout" was created and is available on Google Play for free download. It allows a smartphone user like you to not only browse and search for WiFi hotspot locations, but more importantly, also rate WiFi quality based on your real experience. (Demo'15, Demo'14). It incorporates an incentive and trust engine called "Simple Endorsement Web" (SEW) to link users into an endorsement-based social network to encourage participation and enhance trustworthiness.
